Pocket Forest, Hackney Wick

Hackney Wick, London Remote contributors welcome Started 14 March 2026
About this project

A Miyawaki forest, planted by hand, on a square of asphalt.

Hackney Wick has 7% canopy cover — half the London average. We're turning a 200m² disused car park behind the community centre into a dense, native pocket forest using the Miyawaki method: 30 species, packed shoulder-to-shoulder, that will grow ten times faster than a conventional plantation.

The land has been secured. Council planning has signed off. We have funding for the saplings. What we still need: a few more hands for soil prep weekends, someone to design our launch poster, and a writer to turn our progress into a quarterly update for the local paper.

This is a long project — the forest will take 3 years to establish — but most steps are short, self-contained, and don't require you to be there in person.
